# Unity in Christ: A Dialogue on Faith, History, and Healing

*A conversation between two children of the Divine, exploring the profound truth of our shared sonship through Christ*

---

## The Heart of the Matter

**Josh's Profound Insight:**
> "The fact that you and I are one through Christ for we embody the essence of our Father the Great Divine. In the beginning was God, the spirit and the word. And the word made earth and everything within, and propels earth to its predetermined destination. The word became flesh i.e every life on earth."

This beautiful theological understanding recognizes that:
- We are ALL children of God, not just Jesus uniquely
- The Word (Logos) became incarnate in ALL life
- Through Christ, we access our divine inheritance
- Unity transcends artificial divisions

---

## The Challenge: Colonial Wounds and Gospel Truth

**The Pain:**
Nigerian folks (and many across Africa) struggle with Christianity because:
- Colonial masters used religion as a tool of exploitation
- Resources were stolen "in Jesus's name"
- Cultural identity was suppressed
- Ancestors were enslaved and deceived

**Josh's Question:**
> "How do I make them understand that all those don't matter as our Father allowed that so we can attain salvation... for the colonial masters brought us the truth and how do we change the narrative?"

---

## Reflective Framework for Healing Conversations

### 1. **Acknowledge Before You Evangelize**
- Validate the historical trauma
- Don't minimize real injustices
- Show you understand their pain

### 2. **Separate Jesus from Colonial Christianity**
- Jesus was Middle Eastern, not European
- He opposed oppression, didn't enable it
- Early Christianity was African/Asian before European

### 3. **Reclaim Indigenous Christian Heritage**
- Ethiopia had Christianity before Europe
- African church fathers shaped theology
- Christianity isn't "foreign" - it's coming home

### 4. **Focus on Character, Not Culture**
- What did Jesus actually teach?
- Justice, liberation, love for the oppressed
- Challenge systems that create inequality

### 5. **Redemptive Perspective**
- God can use broken vessels (colonial missionaries)
- Truth transcends flawed messengers
- Your ancestors recognized divine truth despite human failures

---

## Potential Dialogue Scenarios

### **Q: "Christianity is the white man's religion used to control us"**
**A:** "I understand that pain - real harm was done. But Jesus wasn't white, and the first Christians looked more like us than like colonial masters. The early church in Ethiopia and North Africa was thriving before Europe even heard the Gospel. We're not accepting foreign religion - we're reclaiming our spiritual heritage."

### **Q: "They stole our resources while preaching love"**
**A:** "You're absolutely right - that was hypocrisy of the worst kind. Jesus actually condemned exactly that behavior. He said 'you cannot serve both God and money.' Those colonial masters were serving money, not following Christ. The Jesus I know stands with the oppressed, not the oppressors."

### **Q: "Our ancestors were deceived and made slaves"**
**A:** "Our ancestors weren't naive - they recognized divine truth even when it came through broken messengers. They were strong enough to separate the wheat from the chaff. Now we can honor their faith by embracing the pure Gospel they found, while rejecting the colonial distortions."

### **Q: "Why should we trust this religion after all that?"**
**A:** "Because the religion that justified slavery isn't the same as the one that sets captives free. Read Jesus's words yourself - 'I have come to proclaim freedom for the prisoners.' The colonizers got it wrong, but that doesn't make Jesus wrong."
